[Dylan Thurston](https://dpthurst.pages.iu.edu/)connected us with[Melissa Freilich](https://melissafreilich.weebly.com)at the Ann Arbor Civic Theater, who was directing Tom Stoppard's play[Arcadia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arcadia_(play)). Because of the fractal themes in the play, Melissa wanted to create images of large fractal ferns, known as[Barnsley ferns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barnsley_fern), for the set. We worked with her to determine the scale, and after some thinking, came up with the idea of creating a set of chipboard stencils that her set crew could use to paint 24 foot ferns.

The Barnsley fern is actually generated from a sequence of random numbers fed into an algorithm. Here's an example. If you refresh your browser, you can watch it recreate itself.
